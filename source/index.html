Air Quality Forecasts

We present concentrations and depositions for the Northern Europe with 6-7km spatial resolution and 1 hour time step.

The set of substances, which concentrations (upper figures) and depositions (lower figures) are forecasted, includes SO2, NO, NO2, O3, fine particles 2.5 μm, 10 μm.

Wild-land Fire Forecasts

IS4FIRES is built on the basis of active-fire remote-sensing observations following the algorithm described in Sofiev et al, (2009).

IS4FIRES uses SILAM model and utilizes two semi-independent datasets built on the (1) Temperature Anomaly and (2) Fire Radiative Power to forecast AQ during fires.

Allergenic Pollen Forecasts

We forecast concentrations and characteristics of allergenic species over Europe with 20km spatial resolution and 1 hour time step

The forecasted species include birch, grass, olive, and ragweed pollen and are prepared in co-operation with European Aeroallergen Network EAN, which performs Europe-wide monitoring of aeroallergens in the atmosphere.

General Information

The dispersion tools in the SILAM framework allow to choose between the Eulerian and Lagrangian dynamic kernels. The Lagrangian kernel is available until v.4.9, whereas strating from v.4.1 the Eulerian kernel is introduced, based on the advection scheme developed by M.Galperin and complemented with the vertical scheme based on extended resistance-analogy approach.

The chemico-physical modules allow computations of radioactive pollutants (the model database includes ~500 radioactive nuclides with decay chains, dose rates and doses, basic SOx-NOx-NHx-O3 chemistry; size-segregated aerosol compounds, natural allergenic pollutants, production of sea saltp; and probabilistic estimates of plume dispersion expressed via volume- and area-of-risk. Currently, the operational version of the model is v.4.5 for emergency response and 5.1 for other applications.

SILAM is an open-code system and free for research applications. Contact us to obtain the model.
